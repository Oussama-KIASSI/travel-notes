# Switzerland

## Best Time to Visit
- **Alps + Hiking** → Jun–Sep → 15–25°C in valleys; high passes open Jun–Oct; wildflower peak Jul
  - Jun + Sep = less crowded, still reliable weather
- **Skiing** → Dec–Apr → Jan–Feb = coldest + most reliable snow; Mar–Apr = warmer spring skiing
  - Christmas/New Year + Feb school holidays = peak + expensive
- **Cities (Zurich, Geneva, Bern, Lucerne)** → Apr–Jun + Sep–Oct → 12–22°C; ideal walking weather
  - Jul–Aug = warm but busy; Dec = Christmas markets
- **Lakeside (Geneva, Lucerne, Lugano)** → May–Sep → lake swimming Jul–Aug; paddle steamers full schedule
  - Southern lakes (Lugano, Locarno) warmest and most Mediterranean

## Zurich — 2 days

**Attractions**
- **Altstadt (Old Town)** → medieval lanes on both sides of Limmat river
  - Niederdorf (east bank): cobblestones, guild houses, small shops
  - Lindenhof (west bank): hilltop park with views over city + Alps; best in the morning
- **Grossmünster** → twin-towered Romanesque church (12th c); Zwingli launched Swiss Reformation here (1519)
  - Climb Karlsturm tower for panoramic views; modern Sigmar Polke stained glass (2009)
- **Fraumünster** → west bank church with five Marc Chagall stained glass windows (1970) + Augusto Giacometti rose window
- **Kunsthaus Zürich** → major art museum; largest Munch collection outside Norway, Giacometti, Monet, Picasso
  - 2021 Chipperfield extension: Bührle Collection (Impressionists/Post-Impressionists); allow 3 hrs
- **Bahnhofstrasse** → 1.4 km shopping boulevard from main station to lake
  - Luxury brands, Swiss watchmakers, chocolate shops; walk to Bürkliplatz lake promenade
- **Lake Zurich** → swim at public *Badis* (lake pools)
  - **Seebad Enge** and **Frauenbadi** (women-only daytime, mixed evenings)
- **Swiss National Museum** → Swiss history from prehistory through Reformation to modern confederation
  - Castle-like building next to main station

**Food**
- **Zeughauskeller** → 15th-c former arsenal; Zurich's famous beer hall; communal tables
  - *Zürcher Geschnetzeltes* (sliced veal in cream sauce with rösti) = house specialty + Zurich's defining dish
- **Sprüngli** → legendary confectioner since 1836; *Luxemburgerli* (mini-macarons) = Zurich's edible souvenir
  - Café on Paradeplatz; order a box to take home
- **Hiltl** → world's oldest vegetarian restaurant (since 1898); enormous buffet
- **Café Schober** → lavishly decorated Old Town café; hot chocolate + pastries

**Nightlife**
- **Langstrasse** → vibrant nightlife district; bars, clubs, late-night restaurants
  - More diverse than Old Town; **Longstreet Bar** + **Hive Club**
- **Zurich West** → former industrial district (Kreis 5), now creative quarter
  - **Frau Gerolds Garten** (rooftop bar + container garden; top summer terrace)
  - **Rimini Bar** (on the river); craft beer + natural wine bars

## Bern — 1–2 days

**Attractions**
- **Altstadt (UNESCO)** → best-preserved medieval center in Switzerland; 6 km covered arcades (*Lauben*)
  - Uniform sandstone architecture; 11 Renaissance fountains (16th c) with painted figures
  - Sits on a peninsula in a bend of the turquoise Aare river
- **Zytglogge** → medieval clock tower (13th c); astronomical clock with mechanical figures (bears, jester, rooster)
  - Performs 4 min before every hour
- **Bern Minster** → tallest church in Switzerland (100 m spire); late Gothic
  - Main portal: Last Judgment tympanum with 234 painted figures; climb tower for rooftop + Alps views
- **Einstein House** → Kramgasse 49; Einstein lived here 1903–1905 and developed special relativity
  - Small museum preserving original rooms
- **Aare swimming** → Bern's defining summer activity; float down turquoise Aare through the city
  - Enter near Eichholz, exit at Marzili *Freibad*; strong current; confident swimmers only
- **BärenPark** → open bear park on river bank; bears are Bern's founding symbol

**Food**
- **Berner Platte** → Bern's signature dish; massive platter of smoked/boiled meats (beef tongue, smoked pork, sausages, ham hock) + sauerkraut, beans, potatoes
  - Order at any traditional Old Town restaurant
- **Kornhauskeller** → Baroque cellar under the Old Town's former granary; vaulted ceilings with 18th-c frescoes
  - Swiss + Mediterranean cuisine
- **Altes Tramdepot** → brewery restaurant above the bear park; house-brewed beer; terrace view over Aare + Old Town

**Nightlife**
- **Under the arcades** → bars in medieval cellars beneath the arcades
  - **Sous le Pont** (cultural center in railway viaduct), **Dampfzentrale** (riverside venue + bar)

## Lucerne — 1–2 days

**Attractions**
- **Chapel Bridge (Kapellbrücke)** → oldest covered wooden bridge in Europe (1333); 14th-c interior paintings in roof gables
  - Partly burned 1993, restored; Water Tower in the middle; best photographed at dawn
- **Lion Monument** → dying lion carved into rock face (1820–21); commemorates Swiss Guards killed in French Revolution (1792)
  - Mark Twain called it "the most mournful piece of stone in the world"; 5-min walk from Old Town
- **Lake Lucerne (Vierwaldstättersee)** → surrounded by mountains; take a paddle steamer cruise
  - Fleet includes five historic steamers (1901–1928); cruise to Rütli meadow (legendary Swiss founding site, 1291)
- **Mount Pilatus** → 2,128 m; take the "Golden Round Trip"
  - Boat to Alpnachstad → steepest cogwheel railway in the world (48%) to summit → panoramic gondola down to Kriens → bus back
  - Clear days: panorama of 73 Alpine peaks
- **Mount Rigi** → "Queen of the Mountains"; cogwheel railway from Vitznau (by boat)
  - Summit panorama: Black Forest to Bernese Alps; Europe's first mountain tourism destination (since 1816)
- **Old Town** → compact medieval center; painted facades, covered bridges, waterfront promenades
  - Museggmauer (medieval wall; nine towers, four climbable) for elevated views

**Food**
- **Wirtshaus Galliker** → traditional restaurant since 1856
  - *Luzerner Chügelipastete* (vol-au-vent with veal, mushrooms, raisins in cream sauce) = essential order; reservations recommended
- **Rathaus Brauerei** → Old Town brewpub by the river; house-brewed beers + Swiss pub food; terrace

## Interlaken + Jungfrau Region — 2–3 days

**Attractions**
- **Jungfraujoch — Top of Europe** → highest railway station in Europe (3,454 m)
  - Cogwheel train through Eiger + Mönch via tunnels (carved 1896–1912)
  - Observation terrace over Aletsch Glacier (UNESCO; longest in Alps, 23 km); Ice Palace inside glacier
  - Check webcam for clear day; book ahead in summer
- **Schilthorn (Piz Gloria)** → 2,970 m peak via cable cars from Stechelberg/Mürren
  - Revolving restaurant from James Bond *On Her Majesty's Secret Service* (1969)
  - 360° panorama of Eiger, Mönch, Jungfrau
- **Lauterbrunnen Valley** → valley of 72 waterfalls; sheer 300 m cliffs
  - Staubbach Falls (297 m free-falling; inspired Goethe) visible from village
  - **Trümmelbach Falls** → ten glacier waterfalls inside the mountain; tunnel lift access
- **Grindelwald** → Alpine village beneath Eiger north face
  - **First Cliff Walk** → suspended walkway over 200 m drop; access via cable car
  - **Eiger Express** gondola: fastest way up to Jungfrau region
- **Harder Kulm** → viewpoint above Interlaken; 10-min funicular
  - Views of Lake Brienz, Lake Thun, Eiger-Mönch-Jungfrau; best at sunset

**Food**
- **Fondue and Raclette** → essential Swiss mountain food
  - *Fondue*: melted Gruyère + Emmental with white wine + kirsch; dip bread on long forks
  - *Raclette*: half cheese wheel melted and scraped onto potatoes, cornichons, pickled onions
- **Goldener Anker (Interlaken)** → traditional Swiss restaurant; reliable fondue + rösti

## Zermatt + Matterhorn — 2 days

**Attractions**
- **Matterhorn** → 4,478 m pyramid; visible from everywhere in Zermatt
  - Clear views not guaranteed — check webcam; best viewpoints: Gornergrat + Rothorn
- **Gornergrat Railway** → open-air cogwheel train; 33 min to 3,089 m viewpoint
  - Pass Riffelsee (mirrors Matterhorn on calm mornings; arrive before 8 AM)
  - Panorama: 29 peaks over 4,000 m + Gorner Glacier
- **Matterhorn Glacier Paradise** → highest cable car station in Europe (3,883 m)
  - Viewing platform + ice palace inside glacier; clear days: panorama to Mont Blanc
- **Zermatt village** → car-free (electric vehicles only); traditional Valais chalets
  - **Matterhorn Museum** (underground; first 1865 ascent + disaster)
- **5 Lakes Walk** → 9.5 km, 3 hrs; five lakes each reflecting Matterhorn differently
  - Start from Blauherd lift; moderate difficulty

**Food**
- **Walliser Teller** → traditional Valais platter: air-dried beef (*Bündnerfleisch*), dried sausage, aged Alpine cheese, rye bread
- **Chez Vrony** → mountain restaurant in Findeln; terrace with direct Matterhorn view
  - Lamb + handmade pasta; book well ahead; 20-min walk from Sunnegga lift

## Geneva — 1–2 days

**Attractions**
- **Jet d'Eau** → 140 m water fountain in Lake Geneva; visible citywide
  - Best from lakefront promenade or Bains des Pâquis
- **Old Town** → hilltop medieval quarter
  - Cathédrale Saint-Pierre (climb towers; views of lake + Mont Blanc on clear days)
  - Maison Tavel (oldest house in Geneva; city history museum)
  - Place du Bourg-de-Four (oldest square)
- **United Nations (Palais des Nations)** → European UN headquarters; guided tours
  - Assembly Hall + Council Chamber with José Maria Sert murals; passport required
- **CERN** → birthplace of the World Wide Web (1989); home to Large Hadron Collider
  - *Science Gateway* (Renzo Piano design, opened 2023); facility tours: book well in advance
- **Lake Geneva cruise** → CGN paddle steamers to Lausanne, Montreux or Yvoire (France)
  - Views of Alps + vineyards
- **Bains des Pâquis** → public baths on lake jetty; swimming, sauna, fondue in winter, sundowners in summer

**Food**
- **Café du Soleil (Petit-Saconnex)** → famous fondue restaurant; traditional *moitié-moitié* (half Gruyère, half Vacherin); book ahead
- **Buvette des Bains** → café at Bains des Pâquis; fondue on the lake in winter; cheap + atmospheric
- **Filets de perche** → Lake Geneva's signature dish; pan-fried perch with lemon + frites/salad
  - Order at any lakeside restaurant

## Scenic Rail Journeys

- **Glacier Express** → Zermatt to St. Moritz; 8 hrs; 291 bridges, 91 tunnels, Oberalp Pass (2,033 m)
  - Panoramic windows; book months ahead in summer
- **Bernina Express** → Chur/St. Moritz to Tirano (Italy); UNESCO route
  - Landwasser Viaduct, Bernina Pass (2,253 m; highest Alps crossing without tunnel), descent into Mediterranean vegetation
- **GoldenPass Line** → Lucerne to Montreux via Interlaken; three connecting trains
  - Lakeside, Alpine meadow + vineyard landscapes; no reservation required on regional trains

## Tips — Switzerland
- **Language** → four national languages: German (Swiss German dialect, north/east), French (west), Italian (Ticino), Romansh (Graubünden)
  - English widely spoken; attempt the local language of your region
- **Transport** → Swiss Travel Pass: unlimited trains, buses, boats, city transport + museum entry
  - 3/4/6/8/15-day versions; buy before arrival
  - Swiss rail system is extremely punctual and efficient
- **Cost** → expensive
  - Swiss Travel Pass + supermarket meals (Coop, Migros) help manage costs
- **Tipping** → service included by law; additional tips appreciated but never expected
- **Must-eat** → *fondue*, *raclette*, *rösti* (crispy shredded potato cake; Bern origin), *Zürcher Geschnetzeltes*, *Bündnerfleisch* (air-dried beef), *Birchermüesli* (invented in Zurich c. 1900), Swiss chocolate (Lindt, Sprüngli, Läderach, Cailler)
- **Must-drink** → Swiss wine (rarely exported; Fendant/Chasselas whites from Valais, Pinot Noir from Graubünden), *Rivella* (milk-whey soft drink), *Appenzeller Alpenbitter* (herbal liqueur), local craft beers
- **Hiking** → 65,000 km of marked trails; yellow signposts always accurate
  - Grades: yellow = hiking; red-white-red = mountain (some exposure); blue-white-blue = alpine (technical)
- **Safety** → one of the safest countries; mountain safety requires weather awareness above 2,000 m
  - Cable cars + mountain railways have seasonal schedules — verify before going
- **Emergency** → 112 (EU), 117 (police), 118 (fire), 144 (ambulance), 1414 (REGA mountain rescue)
