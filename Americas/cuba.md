# Cuba

## Best Time to Visit
- **Nov–Apr** → dry season; 22–28°C; clear skies; peak tourist season
- **May–Oct** → rainy season; humid 30°C+; hurricane risk Jun–Oct
  - Hurricane Ian (Sept 2022) and Idalia (2023) caused major damage; recovery ongoing
  - Fewer tourists; lower paladar prices
- Havana + western Cuba → year-round; summer hot + wet but manageable with afternoon siestas
- Avoid Jul 26 (Revolution Day), May 1 (Labor Day) unless you want state parades
  - New Year's Eve = anniversary of 1959 Revolution; major national celebration

## Havana — 2–3 days

**Attractions**
- **Habana Vieja** (UNESCO) → 500-year-old Spanish colonial district; founded 1519
  - Restoration began 1993 under Eusebio Leal (d. 2020); key plazas restored, much still in decay
  - Plaza de Armas (original center; daily book market), Plaza Vieja (fully restored), Plaza de la Catedral (18th-c Baroque), Plaza de San Francisco (port area)
  - Walk every street; the contrast between restored and crumbling zones defines Havana
- **Malecón** → 8 km seafront boulevard (built 1901–1952)
  - Locals gather at dusk for socializing, rum, music; walk full length at sunset
  - Winter storm waves crash over the seawall; salt-eroded buildings along the route
- **Capitolio** → restored national capitol (reopened 2019); modeled on Washington D.C.
  - Built 1926–1929; 24-karat gold leaf dome; guided tours available
- **El Gran Teatro de La Habana** → neo-baroque theater on Central Park; built 1915
  - Home of Cuban National Ballet (founded 1948 by Alicia Alonso)
  - World-class ballet performances
- **Museo de la Revolución** → former presidential palace (1920); history told from revolutionary perspective
  - Che's beret + jacket on display; "Corner of Cretins" caricatures Reagan, Bush Sr.
  - Granma yacht (Fidel + Che's 1956 Mexico-to-Cuba boat) in glass case outside
- **Fusterlandia** → Gaudí-esque mosaic neighborhood in Jaimanitas (20 km west)
  - Artist José Fuster has covered 80+ homes, bus stops, benches since the 1990s
  - Donations appreciated; artist often on-site
- **Callejón de Hamel** → Afro-Cuban culture alley in Centro Habana; Santería murals
  - Rumba Sunday at noon (arrive early); touristy but music is authentic
- **Parque Almendares + Bosque de La Habana** → tropical forest along Almendares river; city escape
- **Necrópolis Cristóbal Colón** → 57-hectare cemetery; 800,000+ burials
  - Notable: chess champion Capablanca, Alicia Alonso, La Milagrosa (miracle stories since 1901)
- **Pilar** → Hemingway's fishing boat at Museo Ernest Hemingway, Finca Vigía (15 km SE)
  - Hemingway lived here 1939–1960; house preserved exactly as left
- **Playas del Este** → closest Caribbean beaches (20 km east); where Habaneros go weekends
  - Enjoy the Caribbean beaches

**Food**
- **Paladares** → always eat at paladares (private restaurants) over state restaurants
  - Legalized 1995; restrictions loosened 2011–2021; quality far superior
  - **La Guarida** → most famous paladar; crumbling mansion (featured in 1994 film *Fresa y Chocolate*); try ropa vieja + seafood; book days ahead
  - **El del Frente** → rooftop with Havana views; creative cocktails + Cuban food
  - **Doña Eutimia** → tiny; best ropa vieja + picadillo; 8 tables off Plaza de la Catedral; no reservations; queue before noon opening
  - **San Cristóbal** → Obama dined here 2016; upscale Cuban classics; Santería + revolutionary decor
- **Street food** → croquetas, pan con lechón, tostones, fresh fruit, pizza by the slice
- **El Floridita** → Hemingway's daiquiri bar; frozen daiquiri invented here 1930s
  - Touristy but historic; bronze Hemingway statue at the bar
- **La Bodeguita del Medio** → Hemingway's mojito bar; packed + iconic
  - Graffiti-covered walls (Fidel to Nat King Cole)

**Nightlife**
- **Casa de la Música (Miramar)** → top live Cuban music; salsa, son, timba
  - Two shows: matinee 5 PM, evening 10 PM
  - Dress code after 8 PM (no shorts/flip-flops)
- **Casa de la Música (Centro Habana)** → smaller, more local; matinées
- **La Zorra y el Cuervo** → underground jazz club on La Rampa, Vedado
  - Enter through red phone box; live jazz nightly from 10 PM; arrive early
- **Fabrica de Arte Cubano (FAC)** → converted factory in Vedado; galleries, live stages, DJs, cinema, bars
  - Thu–Sun 8 PM–3 AM; young creative crowd
- **El Sauce** → open-air local venue in Vedado; timba dominates; few tourists
- **Tropicana** → outdoor cabaret since 1939; 200 performers in feathered costumes
  - Kitsch but spectacular

## Viñales — 1–2 days

**Attractions**
- **Tobacco valley** → mogotes (limestone karst hills) rising from red-soil farmland; cycle or horse-ride through
- **Tobacco farm visits** → watch farmers hand-roll cigars; buy direct (better quality + price than state shops)
- **Cave tours** → Cueva del Indio (river cave boat ride; Taíno paintings); Gran Caverna de Santo Tomás (Cuba's largest cave system)
- **Mogotes hiking** → trails through valley and limestone hills; hire local guide
- **Palenque de los Cimarrones** → former runaway slave cave settlement; now cultural center with live Afro-Cuban music

**Food**
- **Casa de Don Tomás** → oldest restaurant in Viñales; colonial house; try delicias de Don Tomás (mixed meat platter)
- **Balcón del Valle** → terrace overlooking mogotes; eat at dusk

## Cienfuegos + Trinidad — 2 days

**Attractions**
- **Cienfuegos**
  - **Parque José Martí** → UNESCO; neoclassical main square incl. Teatro Tomás Terry
  - **Punta Gorda promenade** → pastel Art Deco villas along the peninsula
  - **Palacio de Valle** → Moorish-Gothic-Baroque mansion; now a restaurant
- **Trinidad** (UNESCO) → best-preserved colonial city in the Caribbean; cobblestone streets, pastel houses
  - **Plaza Mayor** → central square; museums, churches, palaces
  - **Museo Nacional de Lucha contra Bandidos** → former convent bell tower; climb for rooftop views
  - **Playa Ancón** → turquoise beach 12 km from Trinidad
  - **Valle de los Ingenios** → UNESCO; former sugar mill valley with plantation ruins + watchtowers

**Food**
- Eat exclusively at paladares in both cities
- **Trinidad** → try canchánchara (aguardiente, honey, lemon, water) at Casa de la Canchánchara on Plaza Mayor

**Nightlife**
- **Trinidad**
  - **La Casa de la Música** → open-air steps converted to nightly music stage
  - **Disco Ayala** → nightclub inside a natural cave; live music then DJ sets

## Santa Clara — half day (en route)

**Attractions**
- **Complejo Escultórico Ernesto Che Guevara** → bronze statue + mausoleum with Che's remains
- **Tren Blindado monument** → armored train Che's forces derailed in 1958; original cars on site

## Varadero + Jardines del Rey — 1–2 days

**Attractions**
- **Varadero beach** → 20 km of white sand; classic Cuban beach resort
- **Cayo Coco + Cayo Guillermo** → remote causeway-accessed keys; flamingos; clear water; best diving/snorkeling
  - More effort to reach but superior to Varadero

## Tips — Cuba
- **Visa** → tourist card required for most nationalities; 30 days (extendable 30 more)
  - US citizens: must travel under 12 approved categories; general tourism not permitted under US law
- **Cash** → bring cash; US cards do not work (embargo)
  - European/Canadian/other cards unreliable; assume cash-only
  - Best currencies: euros, CAD, GBP; exchange at Cadecas or banks
- **Currency reform** → dual system unified Jan 2021; CUP only legal tender
  - Tourist services often quote in USD/EUR; always confirm currency before ordering
- **Shortages** → severe fuel, food, medicine shortages (2020–2024)
  - Causes: US embargo, lost Venezuelan oil subsidies, COVID collapse, mismanagement
  - Blackouts common; bus/taxi availability unreliable; bring medications
- **Internet** → limited; ETECSA NAUTA cards for WiFi at public hotspots
  - Mobile data via NAUTA Hogar SIM; download offline maps before arrival
- **Recent protests** → Jul 11, 2021 largest anti-government protests since Revolution
  - Hundreds arrested; long sentences; travel is safe but tensions remain
- **Must-drink** → mojito, daiquiri (Hemingway version adds grapefruit + maraschino), Cuba libre, canchánchara
  - Havana Club 7 años available in Cuba
- **Must-eat** → ropa vieja, moros y cristianos, lechón asado, tostones, yuca con mojo
  - Lobster at paladares whole grilled; export illegal so all consumed domestically
  - Flan: national dessert; dense and sweet
- **Cigars** → Cohiba, Montecristo, Romeo y Julieta, Partagás
  - Buy from Viñales farmers or certified Casa del Habano shops; avoid street sellers (fakes)
  - Export limit 50 without receipt
  - US import ban: Americans should not attempt to bring them home
- **Classic cars** → ~60,000 pre-revolution American cars still running (embargo froze imports 1960)
  - Classic car taxi tours available; lacking seatbelts and reliable brakes
- **Safety** → very safe for tourists; violent crime rare; watch for taxi overcharging + fake cigars
- **Emergency** → ambulance 104, fire 105, police 106
  - Foreigners must pay for medical care; limited equipment due to shortages
  - Travel insurance with medical evacuation essential
