# Costa Rica

## Overview
Stable democracy; no standing army since 1949; world leader in ecotourism. Smaller than West Virginia but contains 5% of Earth's biodiversity. 25%+ of country is protected parks/reserves. Two coastlines (Pacific + Caribbean), cloud forests, active volcanoes, rainforest. "Pura vida" culture. Highest life expectancy in the Americas. Forest cover restored from 20% (1980s) to 60% (2020s). Tourism is primary industry; infrastructure well-developed.

## Historical Context
Small pre-Columbian indigenous populations (Bribri, Cabecar, Boruca). Spanish arrived 1502 (Columbus's 4th voyage); little gold or labor to exploit. Independence 1821. 1948 civil war (6 weeks) led to new constitution; army abolished 1949; investment in education/healthcare. National parks created 1970s–80s. Oscar Arias won Nobel Peace Prize 1987 (Central American peace). Same-sex marriage legalized 2020. Carbon-neutral goal by 2050.

## Safety & Political Updates (2020–2025)
**Political Situation**: Stable democracy; President Rodrigo Chaves (2022). Strong rule of law. Some poverty/inequality but prosperous by regional standards.

**COVID-19 Impact**: Strict 2020–2021; full reopening 2022. No restrictions as of 2024. Tourism fully recovered.

**Safety for Travelers**: Safest country in Central America. Violent crime against tourists very rare.
- Main risks: petty theft (rental car break-ins, bag snatching, pickpocketing)
- San Jose higher crime than countryside; beach theft common (never leave valuables)
- Rental car break-ins at trailheads: leave nothing visible
- Pacific rip currents dangerous; swim only at flagged beaches
- Don't approach wildlife (snakes, crocodiles)
- Solo women + LGBT+ travelers report feeling safe; same-sex marriage legal since 2020

## Best Time to Visit
- **Dry season Dec–Apr** → best beaches, hiking + wildlife; Pacific coast ideal; most popular + expensive
- **Rainy season May–Nov** → lush, green, dramatic waterfalls; fewer crowds; 4x4 essential (muddy roads)
- **Caribbean coast** → opposite pattern; driest Jul–Oct
- Temp: 25–35°C coast; cooler highlands ~18–22°C (Monteverde, La Fortuna)

## San Jose — 1–2 days

**Attractions**
- **Teatro Nacional** → ornate 19th-c neo-baroque opera house; tour the interior
- **Mercado Central** → covered market since 1880; cheap local food, produce, spices + coffee
- **Museo del Oro Precolombino** → pre-Columbian gold collection under Plaza de la Cultura
- **Barrio Amon** → Victorian + Art Nouveau mansions; street art; creative quarter
- **Volcan Poas** (day trip, 1.5 hrs) → drive near rim; turquoise sulfuric crater lake; can close without notice (check before going)
- **La Paz Waterfall Gardens** (day trip) → 5 waterfalls + wildlife sanctuary; hummingbirds, jaguars, monkeys
- **Coffee farm visits** → Doka Estate + Cafe Britt in Central Valley

**Food**
- **Soda Tapia** → classic *soda* (local diner); order *casado* (rice, black beans, salad, protein, plantains) = national lunch
- **Mercado Central stalls** → *ceviche*, *gallo pinto* (rice + beans with Lizano sauce = national breakfast), *chifrijo*
- **El Patio del Balmoral** → traditional Costa Rican; central location

**Nightlife**
- **Barrio Escalante** → best neighborhood; independent cocktail bars, craft beer + live music
- **La California** → club district; reggaeton + electronic

## La Fortuna / Arenal — 2 days

**Attractions**
- **Arenal Volcano** → near-perfect cone at 1,670 m; lava trails at base (Arenal 1968 trail); sunset + night views on clear nights
- **Hot springs** → **Tabacon** (pools in jungle river), **Baldi** (big complex; good value), **Eco Termales** (quieter, more natural)
- **Rio Celeste** (Tenorio Volcano NP) → blue river; color from mineral mixing at *el teñidero*; 3–4 hr hike; arrive early
- **La Fortuna Waterfall** → 70 m drop; 500-step descent + climb; swim at base
- **Arenal rafting** → class III–IV on Rio Toro or Sarapiqui; half or full day
- **Mistico Hanging Bridges** → 16 bridges (6 hanging) through rainforest canopy; good birdwatching

**Food**
- **La Parada** → popular local restaurant; reliable *casados* + fresh fish
- **Don Rufino** → upscale; steaks + Costa Rican food; good cocktail bar
- **Rancho La Cascada** → open-air; good *gallo pinto* breakfasts

## Monteverde — 1–2 days

**Attractions**
- **Cloud Forest Reserve** → 400+ bird species incl. resplendent quetzal; guided morning tours essential
- **Santa Elena Cloud Forest Reserve** → less visited, cheaper; fewer crowds
- **Canopy + zip-lining** → zip-lining as tourism activity invented here
  - **Original Canopy Tour** + **Extremo** for long runs through forest
- **Night walk** → guided; spot sloths, kinkajous, tree frogs + snakes
- **Hummingbird Gallery** → dozens feeding at close range; free
- **Butterfly + frog gardens** → walk-through exhibits

**Food**
- **Tree House Restaurant** → built around a living strangler fig; try hearts of palm salad
- **Cafe Cabure** → chocolate shop + cafe; Argentine-owned; artisan truffles + hot chocolate
- **Morpho's Restaurant** → local Tico food; good post-hike lunch

## Manuel Antonio — 2 days

**Attractions**
- **Manuel Antonio National Park** → most visited park; beaches + jungle in one walk
  - **Playa Manuel Antonio** → calm, clear water; capuchin monkeys on beach
  - **Playa Espadilla** → main public beach outside park; longer, busier, good surf
  - **Playa Biesanz** → quieter cove; snorkeling
  - Wildlife: sloths, howler monkeys, capuchins, squirrel monkeys, iguanas, scarlet macaws
- **Dominical** (1 hr south) → surf town; world-class waves for experienced surfers; quieter + more local

**Food**
- **Agua Azul** → ocean view; fresh seafood + ceviche
- **Emilio's Cafe** → good breakfasts + fresh fish lunches
- **El Avion** → restaurant inside converted 1954 cargo plane; burgers + local food

**Nightlife**
- **Barba Roja** → sunset cocktails + lively evenings
- **La Cantina** → casual beach bar; sundowners

## Caribbean Coast (Puerto Viejo) — 2–3 days

**Attractions**
- **Puerto Viejo de Talamanca** → Afro-Caribbean beach town; reggae culture; chilled atmosphere
- **Playa Cocles** → best Caribbean beach; consistent surf; black sand; beach bars
- **Cahuita National Park** → coastal rainforest meets reef; snorkeling from beach; capuchins, howlers, sloths; 8 km coastal trail
- **Sloth Sanctuary** → rescue center near Cahuita; two-toed + three-toed sloths; educational tours
- **Afro-Caribbean culture** → Limon province settled by Jamaican railroad workers; English-Creole spoken; coconut milk cooking; reggae + calypso
- **Manzanillo** → quiet fishing village; end of road; Gandoca-Manzanillo Wildlife Refuge
- **Jaguar Rescue Center** → animal rehabilitation; rescued wildlife; morning tours

**Food**
- **Caribbean cuisine** → rice and beans (coconut milk, not gallo pinto), rondon (seafood coconut stew), pati (Jamaican meat pastry), pan bon (fruit bread), agua de sapo (ginger-lime-sugar drink)
- **Soda Lidia's Place** (Puerto Viejo) → authentic Caribbean cooking; massive portions

## Ecological & Biodiversity Highlights

**Biodiversity superlative** → 5% of world's species in 0.03% of land area; 500,000+ species; ~900 birds; 250+ mammals; 230+ reptiles.

**Cloud forests** → Monteverde + Santa Elena; near-constant mist; resplendent quetzals (Jan–May), bellbirds, umbrellabirds; thousands of epiphytes; jaguars present but rarely seen.

**Sea turtle nesting** → Tortuguero (Caribbean): green turtles Jul–Oct (arribadas). Ostional (Pacific): olive ridley arribadas. Leatherbacks at Gandoca-Manzanillo Mar–Jul. Nighttime guided tours only.

**Rainforest regeneration** → forest cover dropped to 20% (1980s); PES program pays landowners to preserve/restore; now 60%+ and increasing.

**Volcanic activity** → ~200 formations; 5 active: Arenal (dormant since 2010), Poas (active crater), Irazu (highest; 3,432 m), Rincon de la Vieja (geothermal), Turrialba (periodic eruptions).

**Corcovado National Park** → Pacific south coast; all four monkey species, tapirs, jaguars, scarlet macaws; primary rainforest; multi-day guided treks only.

**Quetzal habitat** → resplendent quetzal (iridescent green, 1 m tail) in cloud forests; best: Monteverde or Los Quetzales NP; Jan–May breeding season.

## Music & Dance Culture

**Traditional music** → marimba (Guatemala influence), punto guanacasteco (guitar-based; Guanacaste province), paranda (Caribbean coast).

**Afro-Caribbean influence** → Limon province: Jamaican/West Indian roots; calypso, reggae, mento. Limon Carnival (October).

**Calypso** → Walter Ferguson (Gavitt) = legendary calypso musician; English-Creole; Caribbean coast festivals.

**Modern scene** → reggae popular (Bob Marley ubiquitous in Puerto Viejo); Latin pop, reggaeton, salsa in clubs. Costa Rica absorbs Caribbean + Latin influences more than exports.

**Where to experience** → Puerto Viejo for reggae; Limon Carnival (Oct 12) for Caribbean culture; Guanacaste folk festivals for traditional.

## Regional Cuisine Variations

**Central Valley (San Jose)** → casado at every soda; gallo pinto (national breakfast); olla de carne (beef-vegetable soup).

**Guanacaste (North Pacific)** → cowboy (sabanero) culture; rosquillas, tanela, arroz de maiz, vaho; beef prominent.

**Nicoya Peninsula** → "Blue Zone" (longest-lived people); diet: beans, nixtamalized corn tortillas, squash, tropical fruits, fresh seafood.

**Caribbean Coast (Limon)** → rice + beans in coconut milk; rondon, pati, pan bon, agua de sapo; plantain prominent; jerk-style seasoning.

**Pacific Coast** → seafood ceviche, grilled fish, octopus, mariscos.

**Coffee regions** → Tarrazu, Central Valley, West Valley; drink black to taste quality; chorreador (traditional cloth filter).

## Tips — Costa Rica
- **Visas** → most nationalities (US, Canada, EU, Australia, UK) get 90 days on arrival; free; passport valid 6+ months; may need return ticket + proof of funds ($100/month)
- **Currency** → CRC (colon); US dollars widely accepted (change in colones); credit cards accepted everywhere; ATMs widespread; smaller sodas cash only
- **Vaccinations** → none required; hepatitis A, typhoid recommended; no malaria (eliminated 2021); dengue exists (use repellent)
- **Transport** → rental car gives flexibility; 4x4 essential rural/rainy season; book ahead
  - Shuttle buses (Interbus, Monkey Ride, Grayline) available between tourist towns
  - Public buses available but slower; domestic flights (Sansa, Aerobell) for remote areas
- **Wildlife guides** → hire in parks; they spot wildlife you'll miss; available for 2–3 hr tours
- **Manuel Antonio logistics** → book entrance online (SINAC); max 600 visitors/day; sells out Dec–Apr; arrive 7 AM; bring snacks/water
- **Safety** → safest in Central America; petty theft main issue
  - Rental car break-ins: leave nothing visible; use hotel safes; beach theft common
  - San Jose pickpockets near Mercado Central; avoid walking after dark
  - Pacific rip currents: swim only at guarded beaches; don't approach wildlife
- **Emergency** → 911 (all), 118 (fire), 128 (ambulance/Red Cross); Tourist Police: 2-527-7226
- **Must-eat** → gallo pinto (breakfast), casado (lunch plate), ceviche (Pacific coast), chifrijo, patacones, olla de carne, chorreadas (corn pancakes), Caribbean rice + beans, rondon, fresh tropical fruits
- **Must-drink** → guaro/cacique (sugar cane spirit; try guaro sour or chiliguaro with tomato + hot sauce), batidos (fruit milkshakes), agua de pipa (coconut water), cafe chorreado (cloth-filter coffee), Imperial/Pilsen beer, agua dulce (hot sugarcane water), horchata
- **"Pura Vida"** → national philosophy + phrase; used as hello, goodbye, how are you, great, no problem; use it constantly
- **Tipping** → service included in restaurant bills by law; check receipt; extra for good service optional; tip guides; no taxi tips (round up)
- **Power** → 120V; Type A/B plugs (same as US/Canada)
- **SIM cards** → Kolbi/Movistar; data available; 4G in tourist areas; WhatsApp widely used
- **Language** → Spanish; Tico accent drops 's' sounds; uses *usted* almost exclusively; English in tourist areas + Caribbean coast (English-Creole)
- **Water** → tap water safe throughout Costa Rica (rare in Central America)
- **Rainy season reality** → May–Nov; afternoon rain 1–3 PM; mornings sunny; fewer crowds; lower prices; 4x4 essential; some lodges close October
- **What to pack** → rain jacket (year-round), DEET repellent, sunscreen, hiking shoes, quick-dry clothes, binoculars, headlamp, reusable water bottle, light layers (cloud forests cool)
